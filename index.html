Pedro Henrique Braga Lisboa

Research Scientist | AI for Physics & Edge Computing

About

I am a Research Scientist driven to solve fundamental challenges in physics and engineering by merging Artificial Intelligence with high-performance computing. My experience spans from developing real-time signal processing algorithms on FPGAs at the CERN ATLAS Experiment—processing data at 40 million events per second—to creating Deep Learning models for reservoir characterization in collaboration with Petrobras. I also contribute to Petrobras's open-source machine learning tool, the 3W Toolkit.

Currently, I am pursuing a Master's degree in Artificial Intelligence at UFRJ, where my thesis on deep differential logic gates explores their theory and application, serving as a continuation of my experience at CERN by focusing on machine learning applied to nuclear instrumentation and other areas of electronics.

Experience

  • Engineered and implemented Deep Learning models in Python (PyTorch) to analyze microtomography images (Digital Rock Physics).
  • Deployed and scaled these models on High-Performance Computing (HPC) environments using Slurm, Docker, and Singularity to predict complex petrophysical properties.
  • Contributed to the Petrobras open-source 3WToolkit+ by developing modules and baselines to assess ML model robustness for oil-well fault detection applications.
  • Engineered digital filters for real-time compensation of electronic signal distortions in nuclear instruments, focusing on efficient FPGA implementation.
  • In collaboration with the Brazilian Navy, implemented and validated Deep Learning models for automated classification of maritime vessels from passive sonar data.
  • Designed an end-to-end system for monitoring acoustic signals using Python and Qt, integrating machine learning models to support decision-making.
  • Implemented Digital Signal Processing (DSP) algorithms on FPGAs for the trigger system, performing real-time pulse filtering to detect muons at a rate of 40 million events per second.
  • Validated and debugged digital communication interfaces, ensuring data integrity at extremely high transmission rates (7 TB/s).
  • Created a C/C++ software pipeline to process and filter over 40 TB of raw sensor data, extracting more than 1 million events of interest for training Machine Learning algorithms.
  • Performed statistical analyses and hypothesis testing to evaluate and optimize the performance of marketing campaigns.
  • Developed end-to-end systems, including a fraud detection engine, a web scraping system (Scrapy, Selenium), and algorithms for CAD automation.
  • Researched and developed prototypes in emerging technologies like Augmented Reality and Computer Vision for gesture recognition (Python, Javascript, OpenCV, Tensorflow).
  • Developed machine learning models (Python, scikit-learn, Pandas, SQL) to predict product quality in the oil and gas sector using industrial sensor data.
  • Researched and applied Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for novelty detection in passive sonar systems, developing a Python-based preprocessing pipeline for TensorFlow models.
  • Analyzed and preprocessed a large-scale dataset of network traffic to identify usage patterns and trends.
  • Implemented clustering and time series analysis algorithms in R to segment user profiles and model behavioral patterns.
